§ 02 / Featured

Rethinking how we design the components that scientific instruments rely on.

arXiv:2412.09528 · 2024

An MCMC framework for wideband hybrid couplers.

Quadrature hybrid couplers are foundational building blocks for nearly every RF / mm-wave receiver — but designing them across wide bandwidths is hard, and existing methods don't scale gracefully past a few sections.

With Ritoban Basu Thakur at Caltech, I developed a Markov Chain Monte Carlo solver that designs arbitrarily long symmetric quadrature hybrid couplers, producing geometries that outperform published methods for mm and sub-mm wave astronomy applications. The work taught me that real instrumentation gains often come from rethinking methodology, not from incremental hardware.
