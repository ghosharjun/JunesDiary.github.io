§01About

Instruments for fundamental physics.

I am a PhD researcher at the University of Chicago working on precision instrumentation for the direct detection of dark matter, with parallel interests in millimeter-wave cosmology and radio-frequency neutrino astronomy.

My current work spans three threads: axion dark matter searches with tunable microwave cavities at Fermilab, sub-1% dielectric characterization of Greenland firn for the RNO-G neutrino experiment, and the design of terahertz antennas for kinetic inductance detectors in the QUALIPHIDE collaboration. The thread connecting them is a conviction that the next decade's discoveries in fundamental physics will be unlocked by instrumentation that does not yet exist.

§02Research

Current and recent projects.

Direct detection of axion dark matter Active
DEC 2025 —
with Aaron Chou & Andrew Sonnenschein · Fermilab Quantum Institute / UChicago
Analysis of tunable microwave cavities for haloscope-based axion searches, targeting quantum-noise-limited sensitivity across a wide mass range.
Microwave cavity perturbation for dielectric measurement Active
DEC 2025 —
with Eric J. Oberla · University of Chicago
Building a measurement system targeting sub-1% accuracy in dielectric properties, motivated by the need to characterize the upper 20 m of firn at the Greenland Summit for RNO-G.
THz antenna design for kinetic inductance detectors
JUL — SEP 2025
QUALIPHIDE Collaboration · Caltech / WashU / UChicago
Designed and simulated 3.4 THz antennas tuned for the QUALIPHIDE dark photon dark matter search.
MCMC-based design of wideband hybrid couplers
JAN 2024 — FEB 2025
with Ritoban Basu Thakur · California Institute of Technology
Developed a Markov Chain Monte Carlo solver for arbitrarily long symmetric quadrature hybrid couplers, producing designs that outperform published methods for mm and sub-mm wave astronomy.
APSERa cosmology experiment instrumentation
MAY 2024 — AUG 2025
with Mayuri S. Rao & Keerthipriya Sathish · Raman Research Institute
Cryogenic control software, optimized PID system, thermal shroud design and fabrication, antenna and radome simulations, and a simulation pipeline for RF chain non-linearities.
Magnetic fields and rotation of chemically peculiar stars
JUN — SEP 2023
with Viktor Khalack · University of Moncton
Applied least-squares deconvolution to ESPaDOnS spectro-polarimetric data to extract radial velocities, magnetic fields, and refined rotation periods for HD 99563 and HD 210684.
Passive radio astronomy with the CU-ST radar
JUN 2022 — JAN 2024
with A. Datta, A. Patra & B. Paul · IIT-Indore / Calcutta University
Characterized the CU-ST radar, developed a Python spectral-analysis pipeline, and conducted passive tracking of the Sun, Virgo A, and Taurus A.
